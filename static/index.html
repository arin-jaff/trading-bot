In November 2025, Kalshi launched prediction markets on which words Trump would say next. Terms like "Border," "China," and "Tremendous" became tradeable assets. The hypothesis: aggregate every speech, tweet, and Truth Social post he's ever made, and we can predict his vocabulary better than the market.

Edge Computing on a $50 Brain

The entire stack runs on a single Raspberry Pi 4: scraping 10 sources every 2h, SQLite WAL database, nightly model retraining, 2,000 Monte Carlo simulations, and live Kalshi API trading — zero human intervention.

The prediction engine blends 7 ensemble signals: a Markov chain for structural probability, a nightly LoRA-fine-tuned Pythia model for contextual nuance, historical frequency, temporal patterns, and social media velocity via Twitter/Truth Social. When our edge exceeds Kalshi's fees, the bot executes Kelly-criterion sized trades.

The Market: "TRUMPSAY"

What we're trading, how it settles, and what the bot is optimizing for.

Kalshi's TRUMPSAY contract pays $1.00 if Trump publicly says a given word before expiration, $0.00 if he doesn't. Each contract targets a specific term — "border," "China," "tremendous" — including plural and possessive forms.

What Counts
  • Speeches, rallies, press conferences
  • Direct quotes from Source Agencies (NYT, AP, Reuters, Bloomberg, CNN, Fox, etc.)
  • Personal Twitter/X and Truth Social posts
  • Plural/possessive forms ("immigrants" counts for "immigrant")
What Doesn't Count
  • Official acts (Executive Orders, signed bills)
  • Tense variations ("immigration" ≠ "immigrant")
  • Hyphenated/compound words
  • Synonyms or similar words

The Source Agency list handed us our scraping targets — these outlets publish verified Trump quotes within minutes. Combined with his personal accounts, we built a corpus of 56,000+ tweets, thousands of transcripts, and live Truth Social posts. The contract's precise inflection rules shaped our term matching: exact word-boundary matching, no fuzzy NLP needed.

Not all data weighs equally. The ensemble applies a 60-day half-life recency weight — last week's rally matters far more than one from 2017. We also feed back past market resolutions: every settled YES/NO contract becomes a calibration point. This loop — scrape, predict, trade, observe, recalibrate — lets the model sharpen with every market cycle.

The Goal

Price each word's probability more accurately than the market. When our model diverges from Kalshi's price by more than the 4% round-trip fee, we have edge. Buy YES when the market underestimates, buy NO when it overestimates. Size positions with the Kelly criterion, scaled by confidence.
