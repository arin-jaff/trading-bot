The Build Process

The story behind TrumpGPT
Powered by

In November 2025, Kalshi launched prediction markets on which words Trump would say in his next speech. "Border," "China," "tremendous" - all tradeable. I thought that was hilarious, and also that it was probably beatable.

The idea was simple: using transcripts of every speech, rally, press conference, Twitter/X, and Truth Social post Trump gives, you can build a statistical model of what he's likely to say next.

I decided to use a combination of Markov Chains and Monte Carlo simulations to get a good spread for probability distributions of the hot terms. From there we compare those to what Kalshi is pricing to calculate our edges.

The first version ran on my laptop and was very clunky. It scraped a few sources on GPT-2 Small and spit out predictions into a JSON file. It worked well enough that I saw potential, so I kept going.

Then I found out about OpenClaw and other ventures into autonomous agents and LLMs. Instantly I wanted to find a way to run this autonomously, 24/7, without any intervention. The Raspberry Pi 4, 8GB RAM, $50 single-board computer felt like the right level for this project.

The $50 brain

The Pi scrapes 10 sources every few hours, pulls from Truth Social and Twitter, retrains the Markov chain, runs 2,000 simulated speeches, generates predictions, and checks for trades - optimized for only 8GB of RAM. At some point I added Pythia-160M fine-tuning with LoRA, which takes about 75 minutes on the Pi's CPU. Not fast, but it runs at 2 AM during off trading hours when Trump won't speak and affect the market.

The prediction ensemble grew from one signal to seven as I tried to dial into POTUS's patterns. We needed a way to account for his hyperfixations, his targets, current events, hot topics, and more. We had to get into the mind of the most volatile man in the United States.

scikit-learn PyTorch + LoRA FastAPI Gemini API SQLite WAL Kalshi

My model grew to add historical frequency, temporal patterns, trend velocity, event correlation, Monte Carlo simulations, news relevance via Gemini API, and social media velocity from Twitter/Truth Social. Each one catches something the others miss. Finally the bot uses fee-aware Kelly criterion sizing with all the usual risk controls - daily loss limits, drawdown protection, position management.

Is it profitable? Too early to say definitively. The Brier scores look reasonable and the calibration is improving with each model version. Stay tuned to check out the Portfolio value as the model gains confidence.

The whole thing is open source, and has been developed using ONLY free-tier resources and open-source tools/models. If you want to see if you can improve upon it with a better training pipeline or larger model, feel free to fork the repo below!

Demo: Markov chain text generator trained on Trump speeches, tweets, and Truth Social posts
How this works: The same Markov chain that powers the trading predictions can also generate Trump-style text. Under the hood, the bot runs 2,000 simulated speeches per cycle and counts which terms appear - that's how it predicts what Trump will say. This tab lets you see one of those simulations in action. The trading value comes from the aggregate statistics across thousands of simulations, not any single generation.
